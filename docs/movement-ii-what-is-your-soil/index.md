# Movement II — What Is Your Soil?

> **Draft status:** working draft (DEV) — offered for prayer and honest discussion.

Jesus' parable of the sower describes four soils — the hard path, the rocky ground, the thorny ground, and the good soil — and the difference between them decided everything the seed could do. This movement applies that description to a congregation, honestly and safely.

**What this movement holds:**

- **The four soils as a congregation's self-portrait** — hardness, shallowness, thorns (and the parable puts riches and comfort first among the thorns), and good ground; what each looks like at the scale of a whole church, and why a congregation is not simply the average of its members.
- **The hungry-hearts question.** The single most important diagnostic in this book: *do we have soil that includes hearts hungry for more of God?* The instrument offered here is the **Four Connects** — a simple, teachable way of asking how a person connects with God (and noticing when they long for more than they currently have). Used well, the Four Connects do double duty: they *find* the hungry hearts a congregation already contains, and they *offer* those same hearts a path forward. Diagnosis and invitation in one motion.
- **The limits of counting.** Why attendance, giving, and program participation cannot tell you what you need to know — the person who performs the behaviors and the person whose heart is transformed look identical to every counter. What actually reveals soil: patient observation, honest conversation, fruit over time.
- **The guardrails.** This page will be non-negotiable and will sit beside every diagnostic in the book: what a church learns about its people's hearts is held *for* them, never over them. Mirrors for the community, never levers for management. No scores attached to money, position, or standing; no one's interior life reported upward without their knowledge and consent; and one test every leadership must run on itself — *what happened to the last person who told us something we did not want to hear?*

The movement closes at the threshold of action: you know your soil. Movement III is about tilling it — and it starts in a place most churches do not expect.
