# Movement I — Do You Want a Revival?

> **Draft status:** movement skeleton — chapters to follow.

The question sounds devotional. It is actually one of the most searching questions a church can be asked, and this movement exists to keep it from being answered too quickly.

**What this movement will hold:**

- **The question itself** — an opening chapter that asks it pastorally and honestly: what we mean by revival, what we do not mean, and why "yes" must be allowed to cost something before it counts.
- **What the record shows** — the revival model tested against history: how genuine moves of God arose, what they did to the churches that received them, and the drift by which every one of them eventually cooled. (Standalone treatment here; full foundations in *Intentional Journey of the Heart*, Volume 3.)
- **The letters to the seven churches** — the risen Christ's own diagnostics of real congregations (Revelation 2–3): churches praised, churches warned, and one church that had everything but its first love. The question "do you want a revival?" turns out to be a question Christ has already asked churches directly — and He asked it of whole congregations, not just individuals.
- **The honest "not yet"** — what it means, and what it costs, to see the state of things clearly and decide to wait. This movement ends with the reader free: no manipulation, no manufactured urgency. A revival wanted for the wrong reasons is one of the failure modes Movement IV catalogs.

The movement closes by handing the reader who says *yes* — or even *I want to want it* — to Movement II: before you can prepare soil, you have to know what soil you have.
