# A Church in Revival

*A Pilot Proposed*

> **Draft status:** this is the working draft (DEV). Every page here is under construction and subject to change. Nothing in it is a program yet — it is a proposal, offered for prayer and honest discussion.

---

## Two questions

This little book asks a church — its pastors, its elders, its small-group leaders, and any member willing to read along — two questions, and it asks them honestly, not rhetorically.

**The first question is: do you want a revival?**

Not "do you like the word." Most of us do. The question is whether we want the thing itself — God moving among us in a way we do not control, at a pace we do not set, with results we cannot take credit for. Revivals in the historical record are glorious, and they are also disruptive, humbling, and costly. Churches that received them were rearranged by them. So the first movement of this book slows down on the question itself, looks hard at what revival has actually been, and gives you room to answer *not yet* — and to see clearly what *not yet* costs.

**The second question is: if yes, what might it take to prepare the soil?**

Jesus told a story about a sower whose seed fell on four kinds of ground — the hard path, the rocky ground, the thorny ground, and the good soil. The seed was the same in all four. The difference was the soil. This book takes that story seriously as a description of a congregation, and it asks: what is *our* soil, honestly? Are there hungry hearts here — people quietly longing for more of God than Sunday is giving them? And what would it take to till the ground — before the rain comes, not after?

## Where this book comes from

Behind this proposal stands a longer work, [*Intentional Journey of the Heart*](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart/), which spent years cataloging what Scripture says about spiritual cause and effect — including a hard look at how revivals rise, and why they drain away. Two convictions from that work drive everything here:

**A desire for more.** The ordinary believer can hear God's voice in Scripture and prayer and obey it. That is not a claim for special Christians; it is the normal Christian life, and many people in ordinary pews are hungrier for it than anyone has asked them about.

**A recognition of the drift downward.** Every revival in the record eventually cooled. The fire came from God; the cooling followed patterns — recognizable, recurring, and to a sobering degree, preventable. A church that wants revival without studying the drift is asking for a gift it is not yet prepared to keep.

This book is the meeting of those two convictions: the hunger, and the honesty.

## The shape of the book

- **Movement I — Do You Want a Revival?** The question, taken seriously. What revival is, what it costs, and what the record shows.
- **Movement II — What Is Your Soil?** An honest diagnosis — including a simple way to discover whether your congregation holds hungry hearts, and the guardrails that keep diagnosis from becoming surveillance.
- **Movement III — Preparing the Soil.** What a church actually does, starting with its own leadership — and how to build the vessel *before* the rain.
- **Movement IV — Keeping What Comes.** The failure modes of previous revivals, and what keeps the wineskin supple across years and generations.
- **Reference.** The deeper material behind each movement, and pointers into *Intentional Journey of the Heart* for those who want the full foundations.

## A word before you begin

No book produces a revival. God does that, and He has never once been managed into it. What a church *can* do — what churches in the record demonstrably did — is prepare: soften the ground, gather the hungry, form the leaders, and learn the failure modes in advance. That preparation is commanded whether or not the rain comes on our schedule: *"Sow for yourselves righteousness; reap steadfast love; break up your fallow ground, for it is the time to seek the LORD, that he may come and rain righteousness upon you"* (Hosea 10:12, ESV).

If that verse stirs something in you, start with [Start Here](reader.html#docs/start-here.md), and then let Movement I ask you the question.
