# Start Here

> **Draft status:** working draft (DEV) — structure and content still forming.

## Who this book is for

It is written for a church — first for its leadership (pastors, elders, and those who carry responsibility for the congregation's direction), and then for its small-group leaders and any member who wants to read along. You do not need any background in the larger body of work this proposal draws from. Where deeper foundations exist, the book points to them; it never requires them.

## What this book is — and is not

**It is a proposal for a pilot.** Not a program to install, but a path a willing church could walk: an honest season of asking whether we want revival, discovering the state of our soil, preparing it, and learning — in advance — how churches before us received a move of God and then lost it.

**It is not a technique for producing revival.** No such technique exists. The whole record of the church says the fire falls where God wills. The same record says, just as clearly, that the falling fire found some houses ready and some not — and that readiness had a describable shape.

## How the book is organized

Four movements, in order, each building on the one before:

1. **Do You Want a Revival?** — the question itself, with the cost on the table.
2. **What Is Your Soil?** — diagnosis before cultivation, with strong guardrails.
3. **Preparing the Soil** — the practices, beginning with the leadership's own hearts.
4. **Keeping What Comes** — sustainment, and the failure modes of the past.

Then a **Reference** section holding the deeper material: the research behind each movement, a summary of how these foundations were tested, and pointers into [*Intentional Journey of the Heart*](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/Intentional-Journey-of-the-Heart/) — the reference base this book stands on — and [*Fellowship of the Heart*](https://jgtittle-ministries.github.io/fellowship-of-the-heart-pilot-at-cca/), the small-group pilot this book will lean on for its leadership-formation mechanics.

## The order matters

One conviction shapes the sequence of this book more than any other: **preparation starts at the top.** A congregation's soil is tilled from the leadership downward — a leadership that has itself become a fellowship of the heart, before it asks anyone else to become one. Movement III is built around that order, and it is the hardest and most important thing in this proposal.

## Reading time

The four movements are deliberately short enough for a leadership team to read together across a handful of meetings, one movement at a time, with discussion. The Reference section is there for whoever wants to go further.

## For the lead pastor and board

If you carry the responsibility of actually deciding whether your church steps into this pilot, there is a page written for that room: [Deciding Together](deciding-together.md) — a six-meeting path from first reading to an honest yes or an honorable no, with the three questions at the center of the decision and a plain inventory of what a yes commits you to.
